Propulsion

The Propulsion Subsystem propels the pod using two large aluminum wheels, direct driven by Emrax 188 DC Motors. Power is supplied to the motors by several high voltage battery packs, and regulated using the pod’s on board computer. The drag force on the pod is reduced by a lightweight, aerodynamic shell, manufactured in house from carbon fiber and kevlar.

Breaking

Our braking system involves neodymium magnet arrays for inductive braking as well as pads for frictional braking. This system creates up to 1000N in braking force and can slow down the pod in under 10 seconds.

Suspension

The suspension subteam is responsible for crafting the chassis the pod sits on and ensuring the pod is attached to the I-beam track. The suspension subteam also oversees the layout of the internal components of the pod. The design includes a sturdy aluminum chassis frame to provide structural stability, as well as support for critical pod components. Lateral rail followers passively maintain the pod’s direction as well as vertical clamp wheels which keep traction on the central I-beam track.
